TD Bank Capstone · April 13, 2026

Human-Centered AI Assurance

A governance framework and MVP that turns policy into real-time guidance—so teams can adopt AI with clarity, confidence, and care for well-being, privacy, and security.

Explore the solution Meet the team

The challenge

AI is reshaping the workplace—but in regulated environments, adoption brings tension: efficiency and innovation on one side; employee well-being, privacy, and security on the other.

Employees often lack the clarity, confidence, and trust they need to use AI tools responsibly every day—creating inefficiency and institutional risk, not just missed opportunity.

Our industry partner sought a path to integrate AI without compromising a human-centric culture—balancing adaptation with protection.

Our solution

Two pillars work together: an ethical governance framework aligned with employee well-being, privacy, and security—and a project-management integration that puts that framework into the tools teams already use.

◇

Governance framework

Structured assurance so AI developed and used in the organization does not undermine psychological safety, autonomy, or compliance expectations.

▣

Jira-aligned plug-in

Tickets are scanned against the framework; mismatches are flagged with the specific principle breached—making policy operational inside everyday workflows.

MVP decision-support experience

An AI-assisted website guides users in real time: spot potential issues, understand risk, and revise actions—informed by explanations and suggestions rather than opaque enforcement.

Project objectives

  • Characterize the micro-alignment gap in AI safety as it relates to individual well-being, privacy, and security.
  • Develop governance that treats ethical psychological safety and human autonomy as core safety features.
  • Deliver a system adaptable to how each organization defines and applies its own framework.
  • Design a human-in-command path where AI helps uphold ethical guidelines while supporting employees.

How it helps

Violation detection

Surfaces which parts of the framework apply—with links to the right sections so users are not hunting through PDFs.

Explanation engine

Summarizes dense policy into concise explanations to cut cognitive load.

Suggestion engine

Offers revision paths toward compliance—turning a slow manual loop into guided iteration.

Design principles

  • Human-centered design
  • Transparency
  • Efficiency
  • Trust

Compliance through informed choice—not surveillance-only enforcement.

Results & broader impact

What we observed

  • Less time navigating framework documents
  • Better grasp of AI-related risks
  • Higher confidence in decisions

UN Sustainable Development Goals

  • SDG 3 — Well-being: protecting employees from harm and supporting healthier, creative work.
  • SDG 8 — Decent work: ethical guardrails shaped by people, for people—e.g. HR defining protections alongside AI adoption.
  • SDG 9 — Innovation & infrastructure: a foundation so regulated industries can innovate responsibly.

Takeaway

The project addresses the micro-alignment gap in AI safety—how emerging tech touches individual well-being, security, and privacy—through an ethical framework and tooling that scans work items (e.g. in Jira) for gaps against human-centric rules.

It reduces the burden of interpreting complex policy, supports ethical decisions during design, and argues that transparency and guidance scale better than surveillance alone. Built on modular architecture, the approach can extend beyond banking to other high-stakes domains.
